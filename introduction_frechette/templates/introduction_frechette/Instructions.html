  1. In this experiment you will be asked to make decisions in several rounds. You will be randomly paired with another person for a sequence of rounds. Each sequence of rounds is referred to as a match.

  2. The length of a match is at least 20 round, after which it is randomly determined. For each subsequent round past the 20th round, there is a 50% probability that the match will continue for at least another round. This is as if we would flip a coin after each round and continue if tails and end if heads. So, for instance, if you are in round 20, the probability there will be a third round is 50% and if you are in round 27, the probability there will be another round is also 50%.
