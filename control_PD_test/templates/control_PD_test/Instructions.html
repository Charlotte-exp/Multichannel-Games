Instructions

You are about to play a game for money against another anonymous player.

The game is made of at least 20 rounds, after which there is a 50% chance an additional round or for the game to stop. In each round, you and the other player will be given a choice to make. The payoff matrix below shows the possible money you can make on each round based on your decision as well as the other player's decision.

In each cell, the amount to the left is the payoff for you and to the right for the other participant.

The colours signals the different players.

  • Blue signals player 1
  • Red signals player 2

Your payoffs are displayed in Blue too, and the other player's payoffs in Red.

For example:

If you choose Cooperate and the other person chooses Cooperate, you both would receive {{ Constants.both_cooperate_payoff }}.

If you choose Cooperate and the other person chooses Defect, you would receive {{ Constants.betrayed_payoff }} while they would receive {{ Constants.betray_payoff }}.

If you choose Defect and the other person chooses Cooperate, you would receive {{ Constants.betray_payoff }} while they would receive {{ Constants.betrayed_payoff }}.

If you choose Defect and the other person chooses Defect, you both would receive {{ Constants.both_defect_payoff }}.

Once the last round has been played, your payoffs will be cumulated/added up and the total displayed and subsequently payed to you.
